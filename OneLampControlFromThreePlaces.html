Aim

To wire up PVC conduit wiring to control one lamp from three different places using two 2-way switches and one intermediate switch.

Requirements

Insulated Screwdriver

Quantity: 1 No.

Combination Plier

Quantity: 1 No.

Neon Tester

Quantity: 1 No.

PVC Insulated Cable

Quantity: 8 Meters

Theory

To control a lamp from three positions, an Intermediate Switch is connected between two 2-way switches. The intermediate switch contains four terminals and allows the traveler wires to be connected either "Straight" or "Crossed." Flipping any of the three switches changes the continuity of the circuit, toggling the lamp state.

Demonstration

Installation Procedure:
  1. Fix the PVC conduit pipes and junction boxes onto the wiring board according to the layout.
  2. Draw the Phase and Neutral cables through the conduit.
  3. Connect the incoming Phase wire to the center terminal of the first 2-way switch (S1).
  4. Connect the two traveler wires from S1 to the first pair of terminals on the Intermediate Switch (S2).
  5. Connect the second pair of terminals on S2 to the outer terminals of the second 2-way switch (S3).
  6. Connect the center terminal of S3 to the Lamp holder.
  7. Connect the Neutral wire directly to the lamp holder.

Safety & Precautions

  • Always switch off the main supply and remove the fuse before commencing any wiring work to prevent electric shock.
  • Ensure that the Phase wire is always connected to the switches and never directly to the lamp.
  • Check the continuity of the intermediate switch in both positions using a multimeter before installation.
  • Use 24 TPI hacksaw blades for cutting PVC conduit to ensure smooth edges that won't damage wire insulation.
  • Keep an extra 200mm to 300mm of cable at each switch board for proper termination and future maintenance.
  • Ensure all screw connections are tight to prevent sparking and potential fire hazards.
  • Fix PVC conduit pipes securely with saddles at regular intervals to prevent mechanical strain on wires.

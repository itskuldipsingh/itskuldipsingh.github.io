Test the capacior for charging and discharging.

Theory

A capacitor stores energy in an electric field. When connected to a DC source through a resistor, it charges exponentially. The Time Constant (τ = R × C) represents the time required to charge the capacitor to approximately 63.2% of the full voltage.

  • Charging Phase: The voltage increases while the current decreases. Formula: VC = VS(1 - e-t/RC)
  • Discharging Phase: The stored energy dissipates through the resistor. Formula: VC = VS(e-t/RC)

Procedure

  1. Select the desired Resistor and Capacitor values from the interactive controls.
  2. Click CHARGE (Pos 1) to move the switch. Observe the graphs and tables.
  3. Wait until the voltage reaches approximately 12V and current drops near 0 mA.
  4. Click DISCHARGE (Pos 2) to move the switch and observe the energy dissipating.

Safety & Precautions

  • Always ensure correct polarity for electrolytic capacitors to prevent explosion.
  • The capacitor's rating must exceed the 12V source.
  • Always discharge high-value capacitors through a resistor before touching the leads.

Result

The charging and discharging characteristics of the capacitor were successfully tested and plotted. The graphical plots confirm the exponential rise of voltage during the charging phase and the exponential decay during the discharging phase. The time constant (τ = RC) behavior was practically verified.

Aim

To control a single lamp from three different locations using two 2-way switches and one intermediate switch in a PVC conduit wiring system.

Theory

The control of a single lamp from more than two locations is essential in large halls, long corridors, and multi-story staircases. This circuit utilizes the properties of 2-Way Switches and Intermediate Switches.

  • 2-Way Switch: Has three terminals (Common, L1, L2). In one position, Common is connected to L1; in the other, it connects to L2.
  • Intermediate Switch: Has four terminals. It acts as a "reversing switch." In the straight position, it connects the input pairs directly to the output pairs. In the cross position, it swaps the connection of the travelers, effectively toggling the circuit state regardless of the other switches' positions.

Safety Precautions

  • Ensure the main power supply is switched OFF before starting any wiring connections.
  • Always connect the Phase (Live) wire to the switch, never the neutral wire, to prevent electric shock when the bulb is removed.
  • Use a neon tester to verify that no current is flowing through the wires before touching them.
  • Ensure all wire joints and terminal screws are tight to avoid sparking or heating.
  • Wear rubber-soled shoes or stand on an insulating mat while testing the live circuit.

Result

The 3-way control circuit was successfully assembled using two 2-way switches and one intermediate switch. Through systematic testing, it was verified that the lamp could be toggled ON or OFF from any of the three locations independently. The truth table observations confirm the crossover logic of the intermediate switch.

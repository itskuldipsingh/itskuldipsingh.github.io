4. Troubleshooting

Symptom / FaultPossible CausesRepair Action / Remedy
Completely dead (No heat, no lamp)Defective supply cord; Blown thermal fuse; Loose terminal.Check cord continuity. Bypass fuse temporarily to test; replace if faulty. Tighten screws.
Lamp glows, iron does not heatOpen circuit in heating element.Test element continuity. If O.L, replace soleplate/element assembly.
Electric shock to userElement shorted to sole plate (earth fault); Defective earthing.Test resistance Live to Earth. Ensure earth pin is securely bolted to metallic body.
Overheats and burns clothesThermostat contacts welded; Bimetallic strip warped.Test thermostat continuity. If closed (0.0Ω) at high heat, replace thermostat unit.

5. Safety and Precautions

  • Disconnect the main power supply and allow the iron to cool before opening the casing.
  • Ensure the Earth (Green/Yellow) wire is securely bolted to the metallic body to prevent fatal shocks.
  • Never bypass the thermostat or thermal fuse permanently, as they prevent catastrophic fire hazards.
  • Perform an Earth Continuity test using a multimeter before plugging the repaired iron into the main socket.

6. Conclusion

Components were successfully assembled, and continuity testing verified the inverse relationship between element wattage and resistance.
Systematic troubleshooting confirmed that faults are diagnosed via open circuits, ensuring effective repair and safe operational standards.

Staircase Wiring

Objective

"To wire up a PVC conduit circuit to control one lamp from two different places using two-way switches, effectively demonstrating the principle of staircase lighting."

Working Principle

Staircase wiring utilizes two Two-Way Switches (SPDT). The switches are connected such that the Phase wire enters the common terminal of the first switch, and the output travels through two 'interconnector' wires to the second switch. The common terminal of the second switch then connects to the lamp. This setup creates two parallel paths; toggling either switch changes the state of the circuit (Open/Closed), allowing the lamp to be turned ON or OFF from either location.

Safety Precautions

  • • Always disconnect the main power supply before starting the wiring.
  • • Ensure all connections are tight to prevent sparking or heat generation.
  • • Use only BIS-approved wires and switches with proper current ratings.
  • • Keep your hands and the workbench area completely dry during the experiment.
  • • Verify the circuit continuity with a multimeter before applying live AC voltage.

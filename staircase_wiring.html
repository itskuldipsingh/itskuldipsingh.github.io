Aim

To wire up PVC conduit wiring to control one lamp from two different places (Staircase Wiring) using two-way switches.

Requirements

Insulated Screwdriver

Quantity: 2 Nos.

Combination Plier

Quantity: 1 No.

Neon Tester

Quantity: 1 No.

PVC Insulated Cable

Quantity: 6 Meters

Theory

Staircase wiring utilizes two SPDT (Single Pole Double Throw) switches to control a lamp from two ends. The Phase is connected to the common terminal of Switch 1. Two Traveler wires connect the remaining terminals of both switches. Finally, the common terminal of Switch 2 connects to the lamp. The circuit is completed only when both switches are flipped to the same traveler wire.

Demonstration

Installation Procedure:
  1. Fix the PVC conduit pipes and junction boxes onto the wiring board using saddles.
  2. Draw the Phase and Neutral cables through the conduit using a fish wire.
  3. Connect the incoming Phase wire to the Center (Common) terminal of Switch 1.
  4. Link the top and bottom terminals of Switch 1 to the top and bottom terminals of Switch 2 (Travelers).
  5. Connect the Common terminal of Switch 2 to the Lamp Holder.
  6. Connect the Neutral wire directly to the other terminal of the Lamp Holder.

Safety & Precautions

  • Always switch off the main supply and remove the fuse before commencing any wiring work to prevent electric shock.
  • Ensure that the two-way switches are always connected to the Phase (Live) wire, never the Neutral wire.
  • Check all screw connections in the switches and lamp holder; loose connections cause sparking and overheating.
  • Use the electrician’s knife carefully to strip wire ends; avoid deep nicks which can weaken the conductor.
  • Ensure the PVC conduit is securely fixed with saddles to protect cables from mechanical damage.
